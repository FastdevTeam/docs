# Business SMEs / Power Users Guide

These are the people who actually do the work, supervise it closely, or know the edge cases well enough to explain what really happens.

## Why your role matters
- You know how the work actually gets done, not just how it is described.
- You surface the exceptions, workarounds, and approval steps that shape the real solution.
- You provide the fastest feedback on whether a proposed workflow is usable.

## What you own
- The current-state workflow walkthrough.
- Real examples: forms, tickets, spreadsheets, screenshots, and common exception cases.
- Feedback on whether the MVP flow is realistic for day-to-day use.

## Decisions you should influence
- Which steps, approvals, and data fields are truly necessary.
- Which exceptions are frequent enough to include in the MVP.
- Whether a proposed workflow creates new friction for front-line users.

## Where you are needed by phase

| Phase | Your involvement | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 0. Intake | Optional | Helpful if the product owner needs support gathering examples |
| 1. Sales discovery | Optional | Can add quick workflow context, but not required |
| 2. Workflow workshop | Required | This is where your knowledge has the highest value |
| 3. Technical deep dive | Optional | Useful when operational realities affect integrations or NFRs |
| 4. Security / procurement | Rare | Only needed if your workflow includes regulated actions or audit needs |
| 5. Prototype / PoV | Required | You validate whether the thin slice actually works in practice |
| 6. Handover | Optional | Useful to confirm backlog items still reflect real work |

## How to prepare
- Bring 1-2 real examples of the workflow.
- Write down the exceptions that happen most often.
- Note where you currently copy/paste, rekey data, chase approvals, or wait for another team.
- Be ready to explain what "done" looks like for your part of the process.

## What you should not be expected to do
- Decide budget, timing, or vendor approval.
- Commit technical architecture, security answers, or commercial terms.
