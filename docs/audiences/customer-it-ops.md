# Customer IT / Architecture / Operations Guide

This usually means the enterprise architect, integration lead, IT manager, platform owner, administrator, or operations lead who understands customer systems and controls access.

## Why your role matters
- You know which systems are involved and what can realistically integrate.
- You explain the constraints that affect architecture, environments, identity, and support.
- You prevent the team from making delivery commitments that conflict with customer reality.

## What you own
- System inventory, technical owners, and access path.
- Identity, security, environment, and deployment constraints.
- Operational expectations such as monitoring, support, availability, and change windows.

## Decisions you should influence
- Whether the proposed approach fits customer standards and environments.
- Which integrations are feasible now, later, or not at all.
- What access, sandbox, or sample data is required before a PoV or implementation can proceed.

## Where you are needed by phase

| Phase | Your involvement | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 0. Intake | Optional | Helpful when hard constraints are known early |
| 1. Sales discovery | Optional | Useful if identity, hosting, or procurement constraints are already obvious |
| 2. Workflow workshop | Optional | Join when workflows cross several systems or teams |
| 3. Technical deep dive | Required | This is the main session for your role |
| 4. Security / procurement | Usually required | Security answers often depend on your systems and operating model |
| 5. Prototype / PoV | Required if integrations or customer access are involved | Prevents the PoV from failing due to missing access or bad assumptions |
| 6. Handover | Recommended | Confirms delivery dependencies and operational readiness concerns |

## What to bring
- List of systems, owners, and system-of-record boundaries.
- API docs, sandbox details, or the plan for obtaining them.
- Identity requirements: SSO, MFA, RBAC, tenant boundaries.
- Environment and deployment rules, including change windows and support expectations.

## What you should not be expected to do
- Decide business priority or MVP scope without the product owner and sponsor.
- Speak for security or procurement if those functions have separate owners.
