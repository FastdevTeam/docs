# Executive Sponsor / Business Sponsor Guide

If your organization does not use the word "sponsor," this is usually the senior business leader who is backing the initiative, defending its priority, and helping secure budget and internal support.

## Why your role matters
- You create urgency, funding support, and organizational air cover.
- You help the team understand why this initiative matters now.
- You unblock stalled decisions when multiple customer teams are involved.

## What you own
- Business case and success expectations at an executive level.
- Decision path: who approves, who influences, and who can block.
- Escalation when priorities or stakeholder attendance drift.

## Decisions you should make
- Whether the initiative is important enough to staff properly.
- Whether the proposed MVP is good enough to deliver business value first.
- Whether unresolved blockers need executive escalation.

## Where you are needed by phase

| Phase | Your involvement | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 0. Intake | Required | Confirms the business trigger, urgency, and who should be involved |
| 1. Sales discovery | Required | Aligns business outcomes, deadline pressure, and decision mechanics |
| 2. Workflow workshop | Optional, but useful for major scope tradeoffs | Keeps the workshop tied to value instead of local preferences |
| 3. Technical deep dive | Optional | Needed only when technical constraints drive material business tradeoffs |
| 4. Security / procurement | Escalation support | Helps unblock slow approvals or competing internal priorities |
| 5. Prototype / PoV | Recommended for final demo | Confirms whether the PoV proved the business case |
| 6. Handover | Recommended | Confirms the project should move from discovery into delivery |

## What to bring
- The business problem in one clear sentence.
- The main success metric or decision criterion.
- The real deadline and why it exists.
- The names of the people who can approve or block the next step.

## What you should not be expected to do
- Walk through step-by-step workflows in detail.
- Answer deep technical questions about integrations, identity, or hosting.
- Replace the product owner, SMEs, IT, or Security in their specialist roles.
