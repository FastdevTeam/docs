# Customer Product Owner / Business Owner Guide

If your organization does not use the title "Product Owner," this is the day-to-day business lead who can make scope, priority, and tradeoff decisions during discovery.

## Why your role matters
- You are the day-to-day decision-maker for scope and priorities.
- You connect business outcomes to the workflows users actually need.
- You keep discovery from turning into a list of unprioritized requests.

## What you own
- Define outcomes, success metrics, and priorities.
- Provide power users/SMEs to validate workflows.
- Make scope tradeoffs (what is in MVP vs later).
- Help obtain IT/security/procurement stakeholders when needed.

## Decisions you should make
- What success looks like for the MVP.
- Which workflows are in scope now versus later.
- Which open questions need escalation to the sponsor or specialist teams.

## Where you are needed by phase

| Phase | Your involvement | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 0. Intake | Required or delegated by you | Sets the initial fact base and evidence requests |
| 1. Sales discovery | Required | Clarifies outcomes, priorities, and current pain |
| 2. Workflow workshop | Required | This is the main session for your role |
| 3. Technical deep dive | Recommended | Helps translate technical constraints into business tradeoffs |
| 4. Security / procurement | Support | Helps bring the right internal stakeholders to the table |
| 5. Prototype / PoV | Required | Confirms the PoV tests the right workflow and value hypothesis |
| 6. Handover | Required for final confirmation | Ensures the backlog still reflects the business intent |

## How to prepare for sessions
- Bring 1–2 real examples (screenshots, forms, sample tickets/spreadsheets).
- Identify exceptions that happen often (weekly/monthly).
- List the systems touched and who owns them (even if you’re unsure).

## What good looks like
- Decisions are made in the room instead of being deferred indefinitely.
- MVP boundaries are explicit and stable enough for a delivery team to act on.
- SMEs, IT, and Security are brought in before they become blockers.

## What you should not be expected to know
- Detailed integration constraints, identity protocols, security evidence.
Those belong to IT/security stakeholders; we will schedule sessions with them.
