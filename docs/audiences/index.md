# Role-based Guides

Use these guides to brief each participant before discovery starts. Each page explains:
- what the role owns,
- which phases require that role,
- what inputs they should bring,
- and why their involvement matters.

## Responsibility snapshot

| Audience | Main job | Lead phases | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Sales / Account Executive](sales/) | Qualify fit, secure the right stakeholders, and manage the commercial boundary | 0-1 | Without Sales, discovery loses momentum, the wrong people attend, and delivery-grade work can slip into pre-sales by accident |
| [Solutions Architect](solutions-architect/) | Validate feasibility, integrations, constraints, and measurable NFRs | 3 | Without an SA, technical risk stays implicit until too late |
| [Product Manager / Delivery Lead](product-delivery/) | Turn outcomes into workflows, MVP slices, and delivery-ready backlog items | 2, 5, 6 | Without product and delivery leadership, discovery becomes a feature list instead of a plan that can be built |
| [Security / Compliance / GRC](security/) | Run the security and procurement track, evidence pack, and blocker management | 4 | Without security and compliance leadership, enterprise blockers appear late and slow the deal or project |
| [Executive Sponsor / Business Sponsor](customer-sponsor/) | Own the business case, urgency, funding path, and escalation support | 0-1 | Without a sponsor, priorities drift and cross-team decisions stall |
| [Customer Product Owner / Business Owner](customer-po/) | Own priorities, scope tradeoffs, and confirmation of business intent | 1-6 | Without a day-to-day business owner, nobody can make routine scope decisions |
| [Business SMEs / Power Users](power-users-smes/) | Explain the real workflow, exceptions, and usability needs | 2, 5 | Without subject-matter experts, the documented workflow looks neat but misses reality |
| [Customer IT / Architecture / Operations](customer-it-ops/) | Explain systems, access, environments, and operational constraints | 3-5 | Without IT and operations input, integration plans and NFRs are guesswork |
