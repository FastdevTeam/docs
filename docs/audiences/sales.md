# Sales / Account Executive Guide

## Why your role matters
- You create momentum and make sure discovery is tied to a real buying process.
- You secure access to the people who can answer the right questions.
- You protect the team from drifting into unpaid delivery-grade consulting.

## What you own
- Secure an empowered **customer product owner / business owner** and **executive sponsor / business sponsor**, and confirm decision mechanics.
- Keep early conversations outcomes-focused, not feature-list focused.
- Ensure evidence is requested early (screenshots, sample docs, sample data).
- Identify if security/procurement is a gate and start that track early.

## Decisions you should make
- Whether discovery should remain directional or move into paid discovery.
- Which customer stakeholders must attend the next session.
- Whether the opportunity is blocked by missing decision-makers or missing urgency.

## Where you are needed by phase

| Phase | Your involvement | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 0. Intake | Lead | Creates the initial fact base and stakeholder map |
| 1. Sales discovery | Lead | Aligns outcomes, urgency, and the buying process |
| 2. Workflow workshop | Support | Ensures the right customer roles attend and expectations stay clear |
| 3. Technical deep dive | Support | Helps maintain the commercial boundary and next-step clarity |
| 4. Security / procurement | Coordinate | Keeps enterprise review from becoming a surprise blocker |
| 5. Prototype / PoV | Support | Makes sure the PoV answers a real business question, not a vague demo request |
| 6. Handover | Support | Keeps delivery expectations aligned with what was actually discovered |

## What you should *not* do
- Don’t force the customer product owner / business owner to answer technical questions they can’t know.
- Don’t promise scope/estimates before workflows, integrations, and NFRs are understood.

## Your outputs
- Completed initial discovery intake form (or equivalent).
- Discovery summary v0 (what we heard + next steps).
- Stakeholder map (who needs to attend which sessions).
- Risks/unknowns list.

## What good looks like
- The customer knows why each next session exists.
- The right roles are invited before deeper discovery starts.
- The commercial boundary is explained early, not after extensive free work has already happened.

## Commercial boundary (free vs paid)
Discovery can be time-consuming. Your job is to keep early work **directional** and convert to **paid discovery** when deliverables become delivery-grade.
