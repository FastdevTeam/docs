# Product Manager / Delivery Lead Guide

If your team splits these responsibilities, Product usually owns workflow modeling and MVP slicing, while Delivery owns handover readiness, backlog quality, and delivery cadence.

## Why your role matters
- You turn business outcomes into workflows the team can reason about.
- You keep the scope centered on an MVP slice instead of a wishlist.
- You create the handover quality needed for delivery to start without re-discovery.

## What you own
- Translate outcomes into workflows (story map or use-case slices).
- Drive MVP slicing and scope boundaries (in/out).
- Ensure acceptance criteria exists for MVP-critical items.
- Produce a delivery-ready handover packet and backlog.

## Decisions you should make
- How to structure the workflow model so it supports slicing and prioritization.
- Which backlog items are too vague to estimate or hand over.
- When discovery outputs are still directional versus ready for delivery.

## Where you are needed by phase

| Phase | Your involvement | Why it matters |
|---|---|---|
| 0. Intake | Review | Helps spot workflow complexity early |
| 1. Sales discovery | Support | Listens for outcomes and MVP signals that need later shaping |
| 2. Workflow workshop | Lead | This is the main session for your role |
| 3. Technical deep dive | Support | Connects technical constraints back to workflow and backlog impact |
| 4. Security / procurement | Support | Ensures security requirements land in scope and backlog language |
| 5. Prototype / PoV | Lead problem framing | Keeps the PoV focused on one meaningful end-to-end slice |
| 6. Handover | Lead | Converts discovery into delivery-ready artifacts and cadence |

## What you need from the customer
- A product owner / business owner who can make real scope tradeoffs.
- SMEs who can explain the actual workflow and exceptions.
- Examples of current-state artifacts and business rules.
- Clear confirmation of what is in MVP versus later.

## Outputs you own
- Workflow model and MVP slices.
- Prioritized backlog with acceptance criteria.
- Refinement cadence and decision log.

## What good looks like
- The workflow model explains the product better than a feature list could.
- MVP boundaries are explicit and defensible.
- Delivery inherits a backlog with enough context to avoid re-litigating discovery decisions.

## Watch-outs
- A feature list is not a workflow.
- If the product owner / business owner can’t commit to tradeoffs, your backlog will be unstable.
