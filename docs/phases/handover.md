# Phase 6 — Structured Handover + Backlog Refinement

## Lead
Vendor Product Manager / Delivery Lead (lead), Engineering and SA support.

## Recommended timebox
30–60 minute handover meeting + async review.

## Typical commercial model
- **Either:** a lightweight handover summary may be included in pre-sales; **delivery-grade** backlog and refinement cadence is typically part of a paid engagement.

**Convert to paid when (practical triggers):**
- backlog needs grooming beyond an MVP list,
- acceptance criteria/testability must be defined for delivery,
- cross-team delivery planning and dependency mapping is required.

## Purpose
- Convert discovery into delivery-ready backlog items.
- Ensure traceability from outcomes → workflows → backlog items → acceptance criteria.
- Establish ongoing refinement cadence.

## Use this phase when
- Discovery outputs are mature enough to prepare for delivery.
- The team needs one consolidated packet instead of scattered notes.
- Delivery planning, backlog shaping, and ownership now matter more than additional exploration.

## Do not use this phase when
- Core workflow, technical, or security questions are still unresolved.
- The backlog is still too vague for prioritization or acceptance criteria.
- There is no product owner / business owner available for final confirmation when needed.

## Entry criteria
- Relevant prior phase outputs exist.
- Major risks and blockers are visible.
- The team has enough clarity to start structuring delivery work.

## Template(s) to use
- [Handover packet + backlog format]({{ "/templates/handover-packet/" | relative_url }})
- [Decision log + open questions + risk register]({{ "/templates/decision-log-risk-register/" | relative_url }})

## Required customer attendees
- Vendor delivery team (required)
- Optional: customer product owner / business owner for final confirmation

## Agenda
1. Walk through outcomes + scope boundaries
2. Review workflow model and MVP slices
3. Review integrations, data, NFR scenarios
4. Confirm security/procurement status
5. Agree refinement cadence and decision owners

## Inputs to request (evidence)
- All prior phase outputs
- Open questions list + owners
- Draft backlog items with acceptance criteria seeds

## Outputs
- Handover packet
- Prioritized backlog v1 (delivery-ready)
- Refinement cadence and ownership model

## Exit criteria
- Delivery inherits a coherent packet, not fragmented notes.
- Backlog items have enough context for refinement and planning.
- Remaining unknowns, dependencies, and owners are visible.
- A refinement cadence and ownership model are agreed.

## Next likely phase
- Delivery kickoff and ongoing backlog refinement

## Red flags (and what to do)
- Backlog items too large/vague → split and add acceptance criteria.
- Dependencies not captured (integrations/security) → add before estimates.
- No decision owner → risk of churn; secure product owner / business owner involvement.
