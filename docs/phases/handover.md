# Phase 6 — Handover to Delivery

## Practical Meaning
- What this phase is: the consolidation step where discovery outputs are turned into delivery-ready artifacts.
- What it is for: hand the work to delivery without losing the original business intent, constraints, and open issues.
- Why it is needed: without it, implementation starts from fragmented notes and the team re-discovers the same questions again.

## At A Glance
- Who leads it: Product Manager / Delivery Lead, with Engineering and Solutions Architect support
- Typical duration: 30–60 minute handover meeting plus async review
- Commercial model: a light summary may stay in pre-sales; delivery-grade backlog shaping and refinement setup are usually paid
- Use these templates: [Handover packet + backlog format]({{ "/templates/handover-packet/" | relative_url }}) and [Decision log + open questions + risk register]({{ "/templates/decision-log-risk-register/" | relative_url }})

## Minimum Required
- A coherent summary of scope and outcomes
- Workflow and technical outputs from earlier phases
- A first-pass backlog
- Clear owners for remaining questions and refinement

## Optional For More Complex Deals
- Detailed dependency planning
- Expanded NFR and security readiness detail
- Broader cross-team refinement and governance setup

## Use This Phase When
- Discovery outputs are mature enough to prepare for delivery.
- The team needs one consolidated packet instead of scattered notes.
- Delivery planning, backlog shaping, and ownership now matter more than additional exploration.

## Do Not Use This Phase When
- Core workflow, technical, or security questions are still unresolved.
- The backlog is still too vague for prioritization or acceptance criteria.
- There is no product owner / business owner available for final confirmation when needed.

## Before You Start
- Relevant prior phase outputs exist.
- Major risks and blockers are visible.
- The team has enough clarity to start structuring delivery work.

## Who Should Be Involved
- Vendor delivery team (required)
- Customer product owner / business owner if final confirmation or tradeoff decisions are still needed

## What To Cover
1. Walk through outcomes and scope boundaries.
2. Review workflow model and MVP slices.
3. Review integrations, data, and NFR constraints.
4. Confirm security and procurement status.
5. Agree the refinement cadence and decision owners.

## Ask For These Inputs
- All prior phase outputs
- Open questions list + owners
- Draft backlog items with acceptance criteria seeds

## What Good Looks Like At The End
- Delivery inherits a coherent packet, not fragmented notes.
- Backlog items have enough context for refinement and planning.
- Remaining unknowns, dependencies, and owners are visible.
- A refinement cadence and ownership model are agreed.
- The team can start delivery without re-running discovery.

## Next Likely Phase
- Delivery kickoff and ongoing backlog refinement

## Red Flags And What To Do
- Backlog items too large/vague → split and add acceptance criteria.
- Dependencies not captured (integrations/security) → add before estimates.
- No decision owner → risk of churn; secure product owner / business owner involvement.
