# Phase 0 — Initial Discovery Intake

## Practical Meaning
- What this phase is: the short prep step before the first real discovery call.
- What it is for: gather enough context to use live meeting time well.
- Why it is needed: without it, the first call turns into basic fact-finding and stakeholder gaps stay hidden.

## At A Glance
- Who leads it: Sales / Account Executive
- Typical duration: async, about 10–20 minutes for the customer to complete
- Commercial model: usually free in pre-sales; move to paid if this turns into detailed multi-stakeholder requirements work
- Use these templates: [Initial discovery intake form]({{ "/templates/initial-discovery-intake-form/" | relative_url }}) and [Decision log + open questions + risk register]({{ "/templates/decision-log-risk-register/" | relative_url }})

## Minimum Required
- A named customer contact
- A business-side owner or sponsor
- A short written intake response
- Enough context to schedule the next live conversation

## Optional For More Complex Deals
- Early IT, security, or procurement contacts
- Screenshots, sample documents, or example records
- Early detection of hard constraints that may trigger technical or security work in parallel

## Use This Phase When
- You are preparing for the first serious discovery conversation.
- Sales needs enough context to route the opportunity correctly.
- You want customer time in the first live session spent on decisions, not basic fact gathering.

## Do Not Use This Phase When
- The customer has already provided an equivalent written brief that covers outcomes, stakeholders, constraints, and evidence.
- The opportunity is already in deeper workflow, technical, or security discovery.

## Before You Start
- There is a real opportunity worth qualifying.
- Sales has a named customer contact.
- The team needs a baseline view of problem, users, and constraints.

## Who Should Be Involved
- Executive sponsor / business sponsor or customer product owner / business owner
- Optional: IT / architecture contact if known

## What To Cover
1. Explain why the intake matters.
2. Capture outcomes, top workflows, and constraints.
3. Identify systems touched and likely decision gates.
4. Confirm the next live session and who should join.

## Ask For These Inputs
- 1–3 screenshots or sample artifacts from current workflow
- System list + owners (if known)
- Sample data or example records (even anonymized)
- Target go-live date and reason

## What Good Looks Like At The End
- The intake form is complete.
- Baseline problem, outcomes, and context are captured.
- A customer product owner / business owner or executive sponsor is identified.
- Initial risks or hard constraints are logged.
- The next live session and likely attendees are clear.

## Next Likely Phase
- [Phase 1 — Sales discovery call]({{ "/phases/sales-discovery/" | relative_url }})
- In parallel, schedule [Phase 3 — Technical deep dive]({{ "/phases/technical-deep-dive/" | relative_url }}) or [Phase 4 — Security / procurement track]({{ "/phases/security-procurement/" | relative_url }}) early if hard constraints are already visible.

## Red Flags And What To Do
- Customer cannot name an empowered product owner / business owner → request one before deeper discovery.
- Hard constraints appear (SSO, residency, on-prem) → start the technical and security tracks early.
- Success is vague ("better visibility") → ask for 1–2 measurable outcomes.
