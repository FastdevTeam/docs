# Phase 4 — Security / Procurement Track

## Practical Meaning
- What this phase is: the focused track for customer security review, procurement requirements, and evidence management.
- What it is for: surface blockers early and organize the evidence needed to move forward.
- Why it is needed: without it, vendor review arrives late, evidence is assembled reactively, and deals or projects stall unexpectedly.

## At A Glance
- Who leads it: Security / Compliance / GRC, with SA supporting technical answers and Sales coordinating
- Typical duration: async work plus one or more 30–60 minute working sessions
- Commercial model: light screening can stay in pre-sales; questionnaires, evidence packs, and deeper compliance work are usually paid
- Use these templates: [Security / procurement evidence pack]({{ "/templates/security-evidence-pack/" | relative_url }}) and [Decision log + open questions + risk register]({{ "/templates/decision-log-risk-register/" | relative_url }})

## Minimum Required
- A clear signal that a security or procurement gate exists
- A questionnaire, checklist, or required evidence list
- Named owners for customer and vendor follow-up
- A blockers list with next actions

## Optional For More Complex Deals
- Domain-by-domain questionnaire mapping
- Formal go / no-go decisions for PoV or build
- Deeper legal, compliance, or residency analysis

## Use This Phase When
- The customer has a formal security, compliance, procurement, or vendor onboarding process.
- Security evidence or regulatory constraints could block the deal or implementation.
- PoV or data access depends on approval from customer security or procurement.

## Do Not Use This Phase When
- There is no meaningful security or procurement gate beyond light qualification questions.
- The customer has not yet signaled any onboarding requirements and deeper work would be premature.

## Before You Start
- A security or procurement gate has been identified.
- Required customer contacts or questionnaires are available, or requested.
- Sales and SA understand why this track matters for the opportunity.

## Who Should Be Involved
- Customer Security/Compliance (required if they have a process)
- Customer Procurement (if applicable)
- Customer IT / architecture / operations (often required)
- Customer product owner / business owner or executive sponsor / business sponsor (optional)

## What To Cover
1. Collect questionnaires and required evidence.
2. Review data residency and regulatory needs.
3. Map questionnaire domains to evidence pack sections.
4. Identify gaps, owners, and timelines.
5. Decide whether PoV or delivery work can proceed in parallel.

## Ask For These Inputs
- Customer questionnaire (SIG/CAIQ/custom)
- Required certifications or evidence (SOC 2, pen test, etc.)
- Data residency requirements
- Vendor onboarding timeline and steps

## What Good Looks Like At The End
- Required evidence and gaps are mapped clearly.
- Outstanding items have owners and due dates.
- Blockers are visible instead of surfacing late.
- The team knows whether PoV, delivery, or contracting can proceed.

## Next Likely Phase
- [Phase 5 — Prototype / PoV]({{ "/phases/pov/" | relative_url }}) if security allows parallel validation
- [Phase 6 — Handover to delivery]({{ "/phases/handover/" | relative_url }}) when blockers are sufficiently understood

## Red Flags And What To Do
- Security engaged only after commercial close → high risk of delays.
- Mandatory evidence not available → escalate early; adjust proposal.
- Data residency conflicts with delivery model → resolve before build.
